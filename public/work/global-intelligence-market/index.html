The first real constraint in building with AI agents is not the model — it is the compute. GPU access is expensive. Enterprise API subscriptions compound fast. When none of those options are sustainable, the question becomes: what does intelligence actually require? Not a single large model. Not a single provider. A probeable, routeable, health-scored network of endpoints — generators, retrievers, rerankers, verifiers, simulations, deterministic kernels, evaluation services — anything HTTP-callable that improves the quality of an agent's output.

The Global Intelligence Market is that network. It treats intelligence as an acquisition problem: discover endpoints, calibrate them, score their reliability and cost per task, route evidence-gathering work to the best available source, and compose the results. The governing definition of an endpoint is not just an LLM API. It is any unit of cognition, computation, search, transformation, or synthesis that can be probed, tagged, and routed. That framing changes what the system can reach.
